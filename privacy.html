Privacy Policy.

How LOWKI Transport collects, uses and protects personal information under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles.

1. Who we are & scope

LOWKI Transport Pty Ltd (ABN 60 686 744 436) (“LOWKI”, “we”, “us”, “our”) is a Western Australian transport and logistics provider headquartered in Helena Valley, WA, operating depots in Narrogin, Esperance and Tom Price and delivering freight services throughout Western Australia.

This policy explains how we collect, hold, use and disclose personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). It applies to our website, our transport and logistics services, our recruitment activities and our dealings with clients, consignees and receivers, suppliers, subcontractors, job applicants and website visitors. By using our website or services, you consent to the handling of personal information described in this policy.

2. What is personal information?

“Personal information” is information or an opinion about an identified individual, or an individual who is reasonably identifiable, whether the information is true or not and whether it is recorded in material form or not. Examples relevant to our business include a receiver’s name and signature on a proof of delivery, a job applicant’s work history, or a client contact’s direct phone number. Information about companies is generally not personal information, but information about the individuals within them is.

3. Information we collect

Depending on your relationship with us, we may collect:

  • Identity and contact details — name, phone number, email address, company, role and business address.
  • Freight and delivery information — pickup and delivery addresses, consignment descriptions, delivery instructions, receiver names and signatures (proof of delivery), and photographs taken to evidence delivery or freight condition.
  • Account and credit information — credit application details, ABN, trade references, trading history and payment records for account clients (see section 9).
  • Employment information — applications, resumes, licence details and classes, qualifications and endorsements, medical and fitness-for-work assessments, referee reports and right-to-work documentation for candidates; plus payroll, superannuation and performance records for employees.
  • Vehicle and driver system data — GPS positions, telematics, fatigue-monitoring outputs and, where fitted, camera footage (see section 10).
  • Website data — pages visited, device and browser information, and data collected through cookies and analytics (see section 13).
  • Communications — records of phone calls, emails, form submissions and other correspondence with our team.

4. Sensitive information

We only collect sensitive information (such as health information from pre-employment medicals or drug-and-alcohol testing, where applicable to safety-critical roles) with your consent and where reasonably necessary for our functions — primarily meeting our work health and safety and fatigue-management obligations. Sensitive information receives a higher standard of protection and is used only for the purpose for which it was collected.

5. How we collect it

We collect personal information: directly from you, through website forms, phone, email, in person or at interview; from clients and consignors in the ordinary course of providing freight services (for example, receiver details supplied on a consignment note); from referees, licensing bodies and screening providers for employment purposes, with your consent; from credit reporting bodies and trade references in connection with credit applications; and automatically through our website, telematics and vehicle systems. Where practicable we collect personal information directly from the individual concerned.

6. Why we collect and use it

We collect, hold and use personal information to: quote, book, perform and invoice transport and logistics services; communicate about pickups, deliveries, delays and exceptions; verify and evidence proof of delivery; establish, assess and manage credit accounts; assess employment applications and manage the employment relationship; meet legal and regulatory obligations, including Chain of Responsibility, fatigue-management, taxation and record-keeping requirements; maintain the safety and security of our people, vehicles, depots and freight; investigate incidents, complaints and claims; and analyse and improve our services and website. We do not use personal information for purposes unrelated to these functions without consent, unless permitted by law.

7. Direct marketing

We may occasionally send existing and prospective business clients service updates and relevant information about LOWKI’s services. Every such communication includes a simple way to opt out, and we will honour opt-out requests promptly. We do not sell or rent contact lists, and we do not send marketing to individuals who have opted out.

8. When we disclose it

We may disclose personal information to: delivery receivers and consignors as necessary to complete a freight movement; our regional partner carriers and subcontractors where they perform part of a service under our engagement, limited to what the task requires; service providers who support our business (information technology, telematics, accounting, payroll, insurance, legal and debt-recovery providers), under obligations of confidentiality; credit reporting bodies and trade references in connection with account applications, where permitted; our insurers and their representatives in connection with claims; and regulators, law-enforcement agencies, courts or other authorities where required or authorised by law, including under heavy vehicle national law and road-safety legislation.

We do not sell personal information.

9. Credit-related information

When a business applies for a trading account, we may collect identification details, trade references and credit history relating to the individuals named in the application, and may disclose limited information to, and obtain reports from, credit reporting bodies for the purpose of assessing the application and managing the account. Individuals named in an application may contact us to ask which credit reporting bodies we use and how to access their credit-related information held by those bodies.

10. GPS, cameras & vehicle monitoring

Our vehicles operate live GPS tracking, in-cab fatigue monitoring and safety-sense technology, and some vehicles and depot areas are fitted with cameras (including dash cameras and CCTV). Information from these systems — which may include location data, driver behaviour data and footage — is collected for safety, regulatory compliance, fatigue management, scheduling, incident and claim investigation, security, and client service visibility. Access is restricted to personnel who require it for those purposes, footage is retained only as long as needed (longer where relevant to an incident, claim or legal requirement), and monitoring is conducted in accordance with applicable workplace surveillance laws.

11. Overseas disclosure

We do not routinely disclose personal information overseas. Some of our service providers (for example, cloud software platforms) may store data on servers located outside Australia. Where that occurs, we take reasonable steps to ensure the recipient handles personal information consistently with the APPs, including through contractual protections and by preferring providers with Australian data residency where practicable.

12. Storage, security, retention & data breaches

We hold personal information in secure electronic systems and, to a limited extent, in hard copy at our premises. We take reasonable steps to protect it from misuse, interference, loss, unauthorised access, modification and disclosure — including access controls, authentication, staff confidentiality obligations and secure disposal.

We retain personal information only as long as needed for the purposes described in this policy or as required by law (for example, transport, taxation, safety and employment record-keeping periods), after which it is destroyed or de-identified.

If a data breach occurs that is likely to result in serious harm, we will assess and respond in accordance with the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme under the Privacy Act, including notifying affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) where required.

13. Cookies, analytics & third-party links

Our website may use cookies and analytics tools to understand how visitors use the site, remember preferences and improve the experience. Analytics data is aggregated and does not identify you personally. You can control or block cookies through your browser settings; parts of the site may not function fully without them.

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and encourage you to review their policies.

14. Anonymity & pseudonymity

Where lawful and practicable, you may deal with us anonymously or using a pseudonym — for example, when making a general enquiry. For most of our services this is not practicable: performing a freight movement, assessing a credit application or considering a job application requires identifying information.

15. Access & correction

You may request access to, or correction of, the personal information we hold about you by contacting us via the details on our contact page or by emailing sales@lowki.com.au. We will respond within a reasonable period (usually 30 days), may need to verify your identity first, and will not charge for making a request (a reasonable fee may apply to compiling extensive access requests). If we refuse a request, we will provide written reasons and the mechanisms available to complain about the refusal. We take reasonable steps to keep personal information accurate, up to date and complete.

16. Complaints

If you believe we have breached the APPs or mishandled your personal information, please contact us first with the details of your concern. We take privacy complaints seriously: we will acknowledge your complaint, investigate it and respond, usually within 30 days. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may complain to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au or on 1300 363 992.

17. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology or the law. The current version will always be published on this page and applies from the date of publication. Significant changes affecting how we handle personal information will be highlighted on this page.
