WA freight geography

One third of a continent. One accountable network.

Western Australia's freight task is shaped by a simple fact: 2.5 million square kilometres served from one metropolitan corner. Perth's south-eastern freight precinct — Kewdale, Hazelmere, Forrestfield — is where the state's road, rail and air freight converge, which is why LOWKI's headquarters sits in Helena Valley at its edge. From there, three corridors carry most of the state's road freight: south-east through the Wheatbelt to the Great Southern, east and south to the Goldfields and Esperance, and north on the Great Northern Highway to the Pilbara and beyond.

Our depot network mirrors that geography deliberately — Narrogin on the agricultural corridor, Esperance on the south coast, Tom Price in iron ore country — so freight moves between staffed LOWKI facilities rather than disappearing into a partner black hole at the metro boundary. Beyond the depots, established regional partnerships extend delivery into the Kimberley, Gascoyne and Mid West. The result is a single accountable quote and one POD trail, whether the destination is five kilometres from our gate or fifteen hundred.
