Why 24/7 capability changes regional freight

Night linehaul that lands for the morning sort, and hotshots that leave at 11pm — what round-the-clock operations actually mean for clients.

OperationsApril 2026·3 min read·LOWKI TEAM

"24/7" appears on a lot of transport websites. In practice it often means an after-hours voicemail. At LOWKI it means a staffed operations desk, drivers on night rosters and vehicles that leave at whatever hour the freight requires.

The overnight advantage

Night linehaul is the quiet workhorse of WA freight. A trailer loaded in Perth at 6pm can land in Narrogin before the town wakes up, or be well down the Esperance corridor by sunrise — ready for the morning sort, the shift change or the site opening. Roads are quieter, docks are free, and the freight effectively gains a day.

Urgency doesn't keep office hours

The machine that stops a production line rarely fails at 10am on a Tuesday. Our hotshot service exists precisely because urgency is a 24-hour phenomenon: a call to the desk at 9pm can have a dedicated vehicle rolling before midnight, fatigue-managed and tracked live to a mine site or regional workshop.

What it takes to run

Genuine 24/7 operations require night-qualified drivers, fatigue systems, and allocators who can solve problems at 3am with the same authority as 3pm. That's an investment most carriers our size don't make. We think it's the difference between a transport supplier and a transport partner.
