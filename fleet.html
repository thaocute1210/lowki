Fleet philosophy

A fleet is a promise about tomorrow morning. Ours is kept in the workshop.

Fleet strategy in WA transport is a balance between depth and discipline. Too few vehicles and one breakdown cascades into missed cut-offs; too many and idle capital erodes the rates clients pay. LOWKI runs approximately sixty vehicles across four classes precisely because that depth lets us absorb the unexpected — a workshop day, a surge, an urgent hotshot — without touching committed runs. It is also what makes our on-demand service possible: the truck we lend your operation on a bad morning exists because fleet depth is designed in, not improvised.

Every class earns its place against a freight profile. B-doubles carry the economics of long corridors; curtain-sided semis handle the daily bread of palletised freight; tail-lift rigids serve the majority of WA delivery points that have no dock; and light vehicles keep urgent small freight genuinely urgent. Preventative maintenance is scheduled, not reactive — and with half the fleet transitioning to electric over the next twelve months, that discipline now extends to charging plans and duty-cycle design.
