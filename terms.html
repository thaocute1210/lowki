1. Definitions & interpretation

In these terms: “LOWKI” means LOWKI Transport Pty Ltd (ABN 60 686 744 436), its employees and agents. “Customer” means the party requesting Services, and includes the consignor, the consignee and any party acting on their behalf. “Goods” means the items tendered for carriage or handling, including packaging, pallets and containers. “Services” means transport, logistics and related services provided by LOWKI, including express distribution, statewide freight, hotshot delivery, tail-lift delivery, warehousing incidental to carriage, and on-demand driver and vehicle supply. “Dangerous Goods” has the meaning given by the Australian Dangerous Goods Code. “HVNL” means the Heavy Vehicle National Law and equivalent Western Australian legislation. Headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.

2. Application & variation

These terms apply to all Services unless expressly varied in writing by an authorised representative of LOWKI. By tendering Goods, accepting a quotation, opening a credit account or instructing LOWKI to perform Services, the Customer agrees to these terms and warrants that it has authority to bind the owner of the Goods. These terms prevail over any terms proposed by the Customer (including on purchase orders) unless LOWKI expressly accepts those terms in writing. LOWKI may update these terms from time to time; the version published at the time of booking applies to that booking.

3. Quotations & pricing

Quotations are based on the information supplied by the Customer — including freight description, weight, dimensions, quantity, access and timing — and are valid for 30 days unless stated otherwise. LOWKI may re-quote or apply additional charges where the actual task differs from the information supplied, including: incorrect or incomplete declarations of weight, dimensions or freight class; waiting time beyond reasonable allowance (see section 12); futile pickup or delivery; out-of-hours, tail-lift, escort or site-induction requirements not disclosed at booking; changes to pickup or delivery locations; and re-delivery or storage arising from failed delivery. Any applicable fuel levy or surcharge is disclosed in the quotation or rate schedule.

4. GST

Unless expressly stated otherwise, prices quoted are exclusive of GST. Where GST is payable on a taxable supply under these terms, the Customer must pay the GST amount in addition to the quoted price, and LOWKI will provide a tax invoice.

5. Customer obligations & warranties

The Customer warrants and agrees that: (a) Goods are accurately and completely described, including weight and dimensions; (b) Goods are properly packed, labelled and fit for carriage by road; (c) Goods are lawful to possess and carry, and are not stolen or subject to restrictions the Customer has not disclosed; (d) Dangerous Goods are declared in writing before booking (see section 7); (e) pickup and delivery sites are safe, lawful and reasonably accessible for the vehicle type booked; (f) the Customer holds, or will obtain, any consents necessary for LOWKI to collect and deliver the Goods; and (g) the Customer complies with its duties under the HVNL and Chain of Responsibility obligations, including in relation to scheduling, loading, load restraint information and speed compliance. The Customer indemnifies LOWKI against loss, damage, fines or claims arising from breach of these warranties.

6. Packaging & labelling

Goods must be packed to withstand the ordinary rigours of road transport, including handling, vibration and stacking, and be clearly labelled with delivery details. LOWKI is not liable for loss or damage arising from inadequate packaging, unsuitable pallets or unlabelled freight. LOWKI may refuse Goods that are inadequately packed or that appear unsafe to carry, and may repack or make safe at the Customer’s reasonable cost where necessary to proceed.

7. Dangerous & prohibited goods

Dangerous Goods are carried only by prior written agreement and in compliance with the ADG Code, including documentation, placarding and segregation requirements. The following are prohibited unless expressly agreed in writing: explosives, illegal items, and Goods that may harm people, other freight or equipment. If undeclared Dangerous Goods or prohibited items are tendered, LOWKI may refuse, return, unload, isolate or (where safety requires) dispose of them at the Customer’s cost, and the Customer indemnifies LOWKI against all resulting loss, damage, penalties and claims.

8. Perishable goods

Perishable Goods are carried at the Customer’s risk as to deterioration arising from the inherent nature of the Goods or from delay not caused by LOWKI’s negligence. The Customer must disclose perishable freight at booking so appropriate scheduling can be planned. If perishable Goods cannot be delivered, LOWKI may take reasonable steps to preserve, sell or dispose of them, accounting to the Customer for any proceeds less costs.

9. Carriage, subcontracting & personnel

LOWKI may determine the route, method, handling, storage and vehicle used for any task, and may subcontract any part of the Services, including to regional partner carriers, remaining responsible for their performance under this agreement. Every defence, limitation and protection available to LOWKI under these terms extends to LOWKI’s employees, agents and subcontractors as if expressed for their benefit. The Customer must not directly engage a LOWKI subcontractor introduced through the Services, for the same or similar work, within 6 months without LOWKI’s written consent.

10. On-demand fleet & driver supply

Where Services comprise the supply of drivers or vehicles into the Customer’s operation: (a) LOWKI warrants supplied drivers hold the licences and qualifications notified for the engagement; (b) while operating under the Customer’s dispatch, the Customer is responsible for lawful directions, site safety, task allocation and compliance of loads planned by the Customer; (c) the Customer must not direct a supplied driver to breach fatigue, speed, mass or load-restraint requirements, and LOWKI drivers are authorised to refuse unlawful or unsafe directions; (d) supplied vehicles remain LOWKI property and must be used only for the agreed work; and (e) the Customer must report any incident involving supplied personnel or vehicles to LOWKI immediately.

11. Delivery & failed delivery

Delivery occurs when Goods are tendered at the nominated address to any person reasonably appearing to have authority to receive them, or as otherwise instructed by the Customer (including authority-to-leave instructions, which are at the Customer’s risk). If the receiver is unavailable, the site is closed, or access is unsafe or impractical, LOWKI may at its discretion: deliver to a safe location; return the Goods to the sender or a LOWKI depot; or store the Goods — with reasonable re-delivery, return and storage costs payable by the Customer. LOWKI will make reasonable efforts to contact the Customer before exercising these options.

12. Waiting time & demurrage

Quotations include a reasonable allowance for loading and unloading time appropriate to the task. Time beyond that allowance caused by the Customer, consignor or receiver — including queuing, site inductions not disclosed at booking, equipment unavailability or paperwork delays — may be charged as waiting time at LOWKI’s prevailing rates, disclosed on request.

13. Cancellations & futile jobs

Bookings cancelled after a vehicle has been committed, or after it has departed for pickup, may incur a cancellation fee reflecting costs reasonably incurred. A futile job — where a vehicle attends but the freight is not available or the site cannot receive it — may be charged at the applicable rate for the attendance.

14. Transit times

LOWKI will use reasonable endeavours to meet requested pickup and delivery windows. Published and quoted transit times are estimates only, and time is not of the essence unless expressly agreed in writing (for example, a confirmed hotshot booking with a committed window). LOWKI is not liable for loss arising from delay except to the extent caused by its negligence, and then subject to section 16.

15. Risk & insurance

Risk in the Goods remains with the Customer at all times; carriage is at the Customer’s risk unless otherwise agreed in writing. The Goods are not insured by LOWKI unless transit insurance is expressly arranged in writing for the particular consignment. The Customer should maintain its own insurance for Goods in transit and is encouraged to raise transit insurance at booking, where options can be discussed.

16. Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law: (a) all conditions, warranties and guarantees implied by law are excluded; (b) LOWKI is not liable for loss of profits, loss of contract, business interruption or any indirect or consequential loss; and (c) LOWKI’s total liability for loss of or damage to Goods, or arising from the Services, is limited, at LOWKI’s option, to re-supplying the relevant Services or paying the cost of re-supplying them, or as otherwise set out in the applicable rate agreement. Nothing in these terms excludes, restricts or modifies any consumer guarantee or right under the Australian Consumer Law that cannot lawfully be excluded; where liability for breach of such a guarantee can be limited, it is limited to re-supply of the services or payment of the cost of re-supply.

17. Claims

Claims for loss of or damage to Goods must be notified in writing within 7 days of delivery (or, for non-delivery, within 7 days of expected delivery), including the consignment reference, a description of the loss or damage and supporting documentation (photographs, values, packing details). LOWKI may inspect the Goods and packaging in connection with any claim; Goods and packaging should be retained until the claim is resolved. Court proceedings in respect of any claim must be commenced within 9 months of delivery or expected delivery, failing which the claim is barred to the extent the law allows.

18. Payment, accounts & lien

Unless credit terms have been approved in writing, payment is due at booking. Approved account customers must pay within the agreed terms from invoice date, without set-off or deduction. Overdue amounts may attract interest at a reasonable commercial rate and the costs of recovery (including collection agency and legal costs), and LOWKI may suspend Services while amounts remain overdue. LOWKI has a general lien over any Goods in its possession for all amounts owing by the Customer and may, after 14 days’ written notice, sell or dispose of Goods held under lien and apply the proceeds to the debt and costs, accounting to the Customer for any surplus, to the extent permitted by law.

19. Force majeure

LOWKI is not liable for delay or failure to perform caused by events beyond its reasonable control, including extreme weather, flood, fire, road closures, border or access restrictions, industrial action, epidemic or pandemic, fuel or power shortage, and government direction. Affected obligations are suspended for the duration of the event, and LOWKI will take reasonable steps to minimise the impact and resume performance promptly.

20. Privacy

Personal information collected in connection with the Services — including receiver details and proof-of-delivery records — is handled in accordance with LOWKI’s Privacy Policy.

21. Notices, assignment & general

Notices under these terms may be given by email to the addresses notified by each party and take effect on transmission (or the next business day if sent after hours). The Customer may not assign its rights without LOWKI’s written consent; LOWKI may assign in connection with a restructure or sale of business. These terms constitute the entire agreement in relation to the Services and are governed by the laws of Western Australia, with the parties submitting to the non-exclusive jurisdiction of its courts. If any provision is invalid or unenforceable, it is severed and the remainder continues. A failure or delay in enforcing a right is not a waiver of that right.
